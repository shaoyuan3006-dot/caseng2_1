Altered consciousness with dysphagia and NG tube

教案設計者: 崇鈺老師研究室團隊 (Shao-Yuan Hao)

📝 Clinical Background:
A 94-year-old female with a medical history of hypertension, diabetes, dementia, chronic kidney disease (CKD), renal calculi, and anxiety/panic disorder. Admitted on Jan 29 due to fever, dysuria, and hematuria (diagnosed as UTI). The patient is bedridden with impaired consciousness and an indwelling nasogastric (NG) tube for enteral nutrition and medication. On Feb 2, her consciousness partially improved; however, she exhibited agitation (shouting and flailing limbs), leading to a suspected diagnosis of delirium. The physician requested a pharmacist's consultation for appropriate pharmacological intervention for mood stabilization and an assessment of the suitability of the new prescriptions for NG tube administration. During a pharmacy ward round, it was discovered that the family had been administering unauthorized traditional Chinese medicine (TCM) decoctions via the NG tube for "calming purposes" without informing the medical team.
